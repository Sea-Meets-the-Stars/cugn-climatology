### 2026-07-17 (New figure #1: typical January temperature, Line 90, Channel Islands)

**Assignment:** invent a figure showing the typical January water temperature on
Line 90, near the Channel Islands. Answers applied: full 2026 beta set OK,
derived quantities OK, novel layouts OK, **no new external downloads**, code in a
**new module**.

**Interpretation:** "typical January" = climatological January mean → I averaged
the long-term `lt_mean_annual_cycle_depth_90.nc` (the full mean+annual-cycle
365-day field) over the 31 January days. "Near the Channel Islands" = the
inshore stretch (0–200 km) where Line 90 threads between the islands.

**Located the islands on the line** (nearest-approach of each island's lat/lon to
the Line-90 path): **Santa Catalina ≈ 60 km** (21 km off the line), **San
Clemente ≈ 95 km** (23 km off) — Line 90 passes between them, matching Rudnick
et al. (2017). (San Nicolas ≈ 155 km, 57 km off — too far, omitted.)

**Wrote** `cugn_climatology/new_figures.py` (new module per the Q&A;
functions + docstrings tagged "Generated by JXP and Claude"):
- `month_mean_dataset()` — collapse a `mean_annual_cycle` product to a
  climatological month (promotes `potential_density` from coord to var so it
  survives as the overlay; returns a length-1 time dim).
- `figure_january_line90_channel_islands()` — reuses `plot_xz_section` (Dan's
  `pxz.m` port) for the section + σθ overlay + seafloor mask + cmocean thermal,
  zooms to 0–200 km, and marks the two islands (dashed lines + labels).
- Output: `context/figs/new_jan_T_line90_islands.png`.

**Result / sanity check:** January surface temperatures ~14–16 °C in the
Southern California Bight cooling to ~5.5 °C at 500 m, a weak winter thermocline,
σθ = 26 near ~120 m — all physically reasonable for winter. Nicely, the shipped
bathymetry renders the islands as topographic highs: San Clemente's marker
(95 km) coincides with a peak rising to ~130 m, and the high near ~200 km is the
Santa Rosa Ridge (Rudnick 2017: "peaks above 300 m"). No reference PNG (invented
figure); validated by scientific sanity. Module compiles clean; left uncommitted.

### 2026-07-17 (New figure #2: dissolved-oxygen decline at depth, Line 90)

**Assignment:** show the oxygen decline at depth on any line. The request was
genuinely ambiguous, so I **asked** (via the question tool) whether "decline"
meant (a) a temporal deoxygenation over the record or (b) the vertical decline
into the OMZ. The user chose **(a) decline over time**.

**Constraint:** oxygen (`doxy`) exists only in the short-term (`st`, 2017–2025)
products, so I used `st_anomaly_depth_90.nc` (Line 90; well sampled). Trailing
all-NaN padding trimmed (valid 2017-01-01 → 2026-07-10, 353 steps).

**Design (two panels, shared depth axis):**
- (a) time–depth Hovmöller of the inshore-averaged (0–200 km) dissolved-oxygen
  anomaly (cmocean `balance`); the shift from oxygen-rich (red, 2018–2020) to
  oxygen-poor (blue, esp. the 2024–2026 subsurface) is the deoxygenation. Layout
  follows Dan's `ptimez.m` (time x, depth y reversed).
- (b) linear trend of that anomaly vs depth (µmol kg⁻¹ decade⁻¹): negative at
  all depths, **strongest −17 near 110 m**, marked. A dashed line ties the
  max-decline depth across both panels.

**Code:** added `figure_oxygen_decline()` to `cugn_climatology/new_figures.py`
(reuses `_trim_time_padding`; functions + docstrings tagged "Generated by JXP and
Claude"). Output `context/figs/new_o2_decline_line90.png`.

**Caveat (annotated on the figure):** over the ~9.5-yr record the trend is
endpoint-sensitive — the oxygen-rich 2018–19 start pulls the slope negative — so
it should be read as multi-year variability rather than a proven secular trend
(consistent with the bgc note in `context/initial_exploration.md` §8d). No
reference PNG; validated by scientific sanity. Module compiles clean; left
uncommitted.
