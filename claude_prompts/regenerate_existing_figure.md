### 2026-07-17 (Diagnosed & fixed the near-coast white patch)

**Question:** why did my figure have a white patch near the coast and Dan's did not?

**Cause (diagnosed from the data):** the two figures start from different fields.
- Dan's `pxz.m` is driven by `makemapplots.m` with the **un-masked `map`
  (total) field**, which the objective analysis fills edge-to-edge (values exist
  even over the shelf); he then paints the black bathymetry `area(...)` on top.
  Result: color everywhere, seafloor black over it, no white.
- I plot the **shipped NetCDF `lt_total_depth_66.nc` = `maptmem`**, which is
  already **topo + error + date masked**. Its valid data tracks the true bottom
  (verified: at 5 km the column is valid only 10–30 m over a 36 m bottom; at
  10 km, 10–70 m over 77 m; full 500 m only from ~20 km out). `matplotlib`'s
  `contourf` leaves NaN cells unfilled, revealing the **white axes background**,
  and my fine 1-km `topo66` polygon didn't cover that coarse (5 km × 10 m)
  masked staircase — so white showed through between the data edge and the
  bottom.

**Fix applied** in `plot_xz_section()`: set the axes facecolor to black so
masked/NaN cells read as **seafloor** (the physically correct reading, and what
Dan's black bathymetry conveys), plus pad a 0 m surface row so the color field
reaches the top instead of leaving a thin black band above the 10 m bin
(overlay σθ field padded to match). Regenerated
`context/figs/regen_66_map_xz_t_2026-01-11.png`: the white patch is gone and the
near-coast wedge now matches Dan's black seafloor. (Remaining difference vs Dan
is only the intended cmocean-vs-jet colormap.)

Root difference worth remembering for future regen work: the shipped NetCDFs are
the **masked** products, so near-coast/over-shelf regions are NaN by design;
rendering them as black (seafloor) reproduces Dan's look without needing his
un-masked intermediate `map`.
